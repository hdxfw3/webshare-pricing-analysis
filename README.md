## Webshare Proxy Types: What You're Actually Paying For

Before you stare at a pricing page and try to do mental math, you need to understand what each proxy type does. Skip this and you'll overpay every single month.

### Datacenter Proxies (Shared)

These are IPs hosted in datacenters, pooled across multiple Webshare users. Fast, cheap, easy to detect on protected sites. Great for scraping public APIs, search engines, light SEO work, and anything that doesn't fight back hard.

### Datacenter Proxies (Dedicated / Private)

Same sped, same datacenters, but the IP is yours alone. Less likely to get burned because some other customer hammered the same site five minutes ago.

Hosted in datacenters but registered under residential ISPs like Comcast or AT&T. They look residential to target sites but run at datacenter speeds. The middle-ground choice for sneaker coping, ad verification, and account management.

### Residential Proxies

Real IPs from real home connections, rotating through a pool of millions. Hardest to detect, slowest of the bunch, billed per GB. This is where you spend serious money if you're not careful.

### Verified / Premium Residential

Webshare's higher-quality residential pool, ethically sourced and individually verified. Costs more per GB, succeeds more often on tough targets like Amazon, Instagram, and large e-commerce platforms.

## How to Calculate What You'll Actually Spend on Webshare Per Month

Forget sticker prices. The number that matters is your real monthly cost based on your traffic. Here's how to get there in five steps:

1. **Estimate your average request size**. A typical scraped HTML page averages 200 KB to 1 MB. Image-heavy pages or JavaScript-rendered targets can push 3 MB.
2. **Count your daily requests**. Multiply by 30 for monthly volume.
3. **Convert to gigabytes**. Total KB ÷ 1,048,576 = total GB.
4. **Add a 25percent buffer**. Failed requests, retries, and overhead always inflate real consumption.
5. **Match the result to aier**. Round up, never down. Overage charges hurt more than upgrading.

Worked example: scraping 100,000 product pages monthly at an average 800 KB each gives you 80 GB. Add buffer, you need 100 GB. That's the $299 residential plan at $2.99 per GB, total monthly spend of $299. Drop the buffer and pick 25 GB? You'll burn through it in eight days and pay overage rates that wipe out the savings.

## Reading the Fine Print: What Affects Your Real Per-GB Cost

A few hidden mechanics shape your actual bill. None are deal-breakers, but knowing them prevents surprise charges.

**Rotation type**: Sticky sessions hold the same IP for up to 30 minutes, costing more bandwidth on retries. Rotating sessions change IPs per request.

**Geo-targeting**: Country-level targeting is included. City andASN targeting on premium residential may impact effective per-GB rates because tighter targeting means smaller pools and more retries.

**Failed requests still consume bandwidth**: A blocked or timed-out request still counts against your GB allotment. This is industry standard, not a Webshare quirk, but it's why scrapers with bad retry logic burn through plans 30 percent faster than they should.

**Annual billing discount**: Switching to annual billing knocks roughly 10 percent off most plans. If you're committed to a tier for the year, this is free money.
